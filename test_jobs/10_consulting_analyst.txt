Business Analyst — Strategy & Operations
Hallmark Consulting Group | Chicago, IL | Full-Time

About Hallmark
Hallmark is a 300-person boutique consulting firm focused on operations strategy, supply chain transformation, and organizational design for mid-market and Fortune 500 clients in manufacturing, retail, and healthcare. We work with C-suite executives on problems that don't have obvious answers.

The Role
We're hiring Business Analysts to join client-facing project teams. Analysts at Hallmark are core contributors from day one — you'll own workstreams, build analytical models, conduct interviews, and present to clients. This is not a back-office support role.

What You'll Do
- Structure and solve ambiguous business problems by breaking them into hypotheses and identifying the right analyses to test each one
- Build financial and operational models in Excel to size opportunities, model scenarios, and support recommendations
- Conduct primary research: client interviews, benchmarking, process observation, survey design and analysis
- Develop clear, concise slide decks and memos to communicate findings and recommendations to senior client stakeholders
- Manage day-to-day project logistics: meeting scheduling, data requests, workplan tracking under project manager guidance
- Operate across industries and functional areas — each 3–6 month engagement is different

Required:
- Bachelor's degree in any field with a strong academic record
- Excellent quantitative reasoning: ability to build structured Excel models, analyze data, and draw defensible conclusions
- Strong written and oral communication; able to simplify complexity without losing accuracy
- Structured thinking: approach problems in an organized, hypothesis-driven way
- High intellectual curiosity; comfortable asking "why" until you understand a problem deeply

Preferred:
- Internship experience in consulting, banking, operations, or strategy
- Exposure to process improvement methods (lean, six sigma) or project management frameworks
- Proficiency in PowerPoint and Excel; Tableau, Python, or SQL a plus
- Experience presenting findings to non-technical audiences

Compensation: $80,000–$92,000 base + performance bonus + benefits. Structured two-year analyst program with associate promotion pathway.
