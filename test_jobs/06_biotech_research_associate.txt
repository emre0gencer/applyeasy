Research Associate I — Immunology (Cancer Immunotherapy)
Pyrex Biosciences | South San Francisco, CA | Full-Time, On-Site

About Pyrex Biosciences
Pyrex is a Series C-stage biotech company developing next-generation cell therapies and bispecific antibodies for solid tumor cancers. We have two programs in IND-enabling studies and a robust discovery pipeline. Our 65-person team spans biology, protein engineering, and translational sciences. We are rigorous, collaborative, and deeply focused on doing work that eventually reaches patients.

The Role
We are looking for a Research Associate I to join our Tumor Immunology group. You'll work under senior scientists on experiments that characterize immune cell function, tumor-immune interactions, and the mechanisms of our therapeutic candidates in vitro and in vivo. This is a bench-heavy role: you will spend most of your time generating data, and we expect you to do it with care and reproducibility.

What You'll Do
- Design and execute cell-based assays to characterize T-cell and NK-cell function: cytotoxicity assays, cytokine secretion panels (ELISA, MSD), flow cytometry immunophenotyping
- Culture and maintain human primary immune cell lines (PBMCs, T cells, NK cells) and tumor cell lines under BSL-2 conditions
- Perform CRISPR-Cas9-based genetic perturbations in primary T cells and cell lines for functional screening experiments
- Analyze NGS and flow cytometry data using Python/R scripts; contribute to data visualization and result summaries for team meetings
- Maintain accurate, complete lab notebook records following GLP principles; prepare experimental summaries for research reports
- Troubleshoot assay performance issues; propose and test protocol modifications
- Support in vivo mouse study coordination: sample collection, processing, and downstream analysis

What We're Looking For
Required:
- Bachelor's or Master's degree in Biology, Biochemistry, Immunology, Biomedical Engineering, or a closely related life science field
- Hands-on experience with mammalian cell culture (aseptic technique, primary cell handling preferred)
- Experience with flow cytometry: panel design, instrument operation (BD LSRII, Attune, or similar), and data analysis (FlowJo or equivalent)
- Familiarity with standard molecular biology techniques: PCR, Western blot, ELISA
- Organized and detail-oriented; ability to run multiple experiments in parallel with minimal supervision

Preferred:
- Experience with CRISPR-Cas9 in primary immune cells or cell lines
- Exposure to NGS library preparation or computational analysis of sequencing data (bulk or single-cell RNA-seq)
- Familiarity with Python or R for data processing and visualization
- Experience in a cancer immunology, CAR-T, or checkpoint biology research environment
- Industry research internship experience

What We Value
We don't care about publication count for this role. We care about whether you can run a clean experiment, troubleshoot intelligently, and communicate your results honestly — including negative results.

Compensation

- Annual bonus (target 10%)
- Equity participation in a Series C company
- Comprehensive benefits: medical, dental, vision, 401(k) with 4% match
- Relocation assistance available for out-of-area candidates
