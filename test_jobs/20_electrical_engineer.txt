Electrical Engineer I — Power Electronics & Embedded Systems
Lumina Energy Systems | Austin, TX | Full-Time, On-Site

About Lumina
Lumina Energy Systems designs and manufactures power electronics hardware for commercial EV charging infrastructure and behind-the-meter battery storage. We are a 90-person hardware startup with Series B funding and units deployed in 8 states. Our hardware team owns everything from schematic to production validation.

The Role
We're hiring an Electrical Engineer I to work on power conversion hardware — DC-DC converters, grid-tied inverter control boards, and the embedded systems that run them. This role is hands-on and spans design, validation, and manufacturing bring-up.

What You'll Do
- Design and review analog and digital circuit schematics for power electronics applications (switching converters, gate driver circuits, signal conditioning, communication interfaces) in Altium Designer
- Perform board-level bring-up and debug of prototype PCBAs using oscilloscopes, power analyzers, and bench instruments
- Write and test embedded firmware in C for STM32 microcontrollers: peripheral drivers (SPI, I2C, UART, CAN), control loop implementation, and fault handling state machines
- Develop hardware test plans and execute characterization tests: efficiency curves, thermal performance, EMI pre-compliance, and power quality measurements
- Collaborate with mechanical and thermal engineers on packaging constraints and thermal management design
- Support design-for-manufacture reviews with contract manufacturing partners; generate production test procedures and acceptance criteria

Required:
- Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering
- Proficiency in analog circuit design and simulation (LTspice or equivalent)
- Experience with PCB schematic and layout in Altium Designer, KiCad, or equivalent EDA tool
- Embedded C programming experience on microcontrollers (STM32, PIC, or similar)
- Hands-on lab skills: oscilloscope, DMM, bench power supplies, signal generator; comfortable debugging hardware at the component level

Preferred:
- Experience with power electronics topologies: buck, boost, full-bridge, LLC resonant converters
- Familiarity with motor control or grid-tied inverter control loops (PID, FOC)
- Exposure to communication protocols used in energy applications: CAN, Modbus, SunSpec, OCPP
- EMC/EMI test experience or familiarity with FCC/CE compliance testing
- Internship or project experience in power electronics, EV, solar, or energy storage hardware

Compensation: $82,000–$98,000 base + equity + full benefits. Fast-growing Series B company with direct hardware ownership from day one.
