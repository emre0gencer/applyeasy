Environmental Scientist / EHS Analyst I
Clearwater Environmental Consulting | Portland, OR | Full-Time

About Clearwater
Clearwater is a 120-person environmental and EHS consulting firm serving industrial, municipal, and real estate clients in the Pacific Northwest. Our practice areas include Phase I/II site assessments, hazardous waste management, air quality permitting, and industrial stormwater compliance.

The Role
We're hiring an entry-level Environmental Scientist to support field and regulatory work across our Site Assessment and Industrial Compliance practice groups.

What You'll Do
- Conduct Phase I Environmental Site Assessments (ESAs) following ASTM E1527-21 standards: records research, site reconnaissance, report writing
- Support Phase II ESA fieldwork: soil and groundwater sampling, boring oversight, chain-of-custody documentation, and laboratory coordination
- Prepare and submit regulatory reports: stormwater pollution prevention plans (SWPPPs), annual hazardous waste reports, air permit compliance reports
- Perform data review and QA/QC for environmental sampling results; manage sample tracking in spreadsheets and project management platforms
- Assist clients with EHS compliance: OSHA recordkeeping, spill prevention plans (SPCCs), industrial stormwater permit inspections
- Write clear, technically accurate sections of environmental reports and permit applications

Required:
- Bachelor's degree in Environmental Science, Environmental Engineering, Geology, Chemistry, or a related field
- Strong technical writing skills; ability to produce accurate, well-organized reports with minimal editing
- Valid driver's license; willingness to travel within the Pacific Northwest for field assignments (~30% of time)
- HAZWOPER 40-hour certification (or willingness to obtain within 90 days of hire — company-sponsored)
- Detail-oriented and organized; environmental data management requires precision

Preferred:
- Internship experience in environmental consulting, industrial EHS, or government environmental agency
- Familiarity with Phase I/II ESA methodology, ASTM standards, or regulatory frameworks (CERCLA, RCRA, CWA, NPDES)
- GIS skills (ArcGIS or QGIS) for site mapping and data visualization
- Experience with environmental data management systems (EQuIS, EarthSoft) or AutoCAD for site figures
- 40-hour HAZWOPER certification already held

Compensation: $55,000–$68,000 base + mileage reimbursement + annual bonus. HAZWOPER and professional certification support (REM, PE). Full benefits.
