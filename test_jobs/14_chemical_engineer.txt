Process Engineer I — Specialty Chemicals
Vantage Chemical Co. | Houston, TX | Full-Time, On-Site

About Vantage Chemical
Vantage is a specialty chemicals manufacturer producing polymer additives, surfactants, and performance coatings for industrial and consumer applications. We operate 3 production facilities in Texas and Louisiana with combined capacity of 400M lbs/year. Our process engineering team owns plant optimization, new product scale-up, and capital project execution.

The Role
We're hiring a Process Engineer I to support operations at our Baytown facility and lead process improvement and capital project work. You'll work alongside experienced engineers in a hands-on plant environment.

What You'll Do
- Develop and optimize operating procedures for batch and continuous chemical processes; conduct process capability studies and debottlenecking analyses
- Use Aspen Plus or HYSYS for process simulation and heat and material balance calculations to evaluate process changes and new product introductions
- Support PHA/HAZOP studies and MOC (Management of Change) processes; ensure changes comply with PSM/OSHA requirements
- Lead root-cause analysis investigations for process upsets or quality deviations using fishbone diagrams, 5-why, and fault tree methods
- Manage small capital projects ($100K–$2M): scope development, P&ID review, equipment specification, vendor interaction, commissioning support
- Analyze production data using PI System (OSIsoft) historian; identify trends and develop KPIs for yield, cycle time, and energy use

Required:
- Bachelor's degree in Chemical Engineering
- Understanding of core ChE fundamentals: thermodynamics, reaction kinetics, heat and mass transfer, process control
- Familiarity with process simulation software (Aspen Plus, HYSYS, or equivalent)
- Strong analytical skills; ability to use production data to drive engineering decisions
- Safety-first mindset; understanding of PSM/process safety fundamentals

Preferred:
- Internship or co-op experience in a chemical, petrochemical, or related manufacturing environment
- Exposure to PI System or equivalent process historian
- Familiarity with PHA/HAZOP methodology or Six Sigma tools
- SolidWorks or AutoCAD experience for equipment specification

Compensation: $72,000–$88,000 base + annual bonus + relocation assistance. PE exam support provided.
